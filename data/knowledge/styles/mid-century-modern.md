# Mid-Century Modern Guide

Mid-century modern (roughly 1950s–1960s) combines optimism, clean geometry, and organic curves. Interiors prioritize open flow, sculptural furniture, and balanced contrast.

## Signature Language

- Tapered legs and floating forms.
- Walnut, teak, and warm woods.
- Organic silhouettes mixed with linear architecture.
- Minimal ornament; form and proportion carry the design.

## Color Strategy

Use neutral foundations with controlled bold accents.

- Base: warm white, beige, soft grey.
- Core wood tone: walnut/teak.
- Accent colors: mustard, burnt orange, olive, teal, petrol blue.
- Keep bold colors concentrated in 10–20% of the visible palette.

## Materials Mix

- Natural: wood, leather, wool.
- Manufactured: molded plywood, fiberglass, metal.
- Recommended approach: pair one “warm” material with one “cool” material in each vignette.

## Lighting

Statement fixtures are part of the style identity:

- Sputnik chandeliers for social zones.
- Arc lamps for seating clusters.
- Globe table lamps to soften corners.

Keep ambient lighting warm and avoid harsh cool-white downlight dominance.

## Furniture Planning

- Anchor with one strong sofa form.
- Add one iconic accent chair shape.
- Use low media/storage furniture to maintain horizontal continuity.
- Favor open-leg pieces to preserve spatial flow.

## Open Plan Philosophy

Mid-century spaces often merge zones. Define areas with:

- Rugs sized to each zone.
- Lighting islands (pendant over dining, floor lamp over lounge).
- Furniture orientation, not walls.

## What to Avoid

- Overly ornate traditional detailing.
- Too much black and white contrast with no warm wood.
- Cluttered surfaces that hide furniture silhouettes.
- Faux-retro overload; blend period references with contemporary restraint.
