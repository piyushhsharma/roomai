# Bohemian Interior Design Guide

Bohemian design is expressive, layered, and personal. It blends global references, handmade textures, and collected objects into a cohesive “organized abundance.”

## Design DNA

- Layering over matching sets.
- Story-driven objects: travel finds, vintage pieces, handmade craft.
- Rich textile presence and tactile depth.
- Eclectic composition with intentional rhythm.

## Color and Pattern

- Jewel tones: sapphire, emerald, burgundy, terracotta, mustard.
- Neutrals still matter: cream, camel, warm white as visual rest.
- Pattern sources: Moroccan, Turkish, Indian, kilim, ikat, tribal geometrics.

Pattern mixing rule:

- Vary scale (large + medium + small).
- Keep one shared color across patterns.
- Use solids between patterned surfaces to avoid visual fatigue.

## Materials and Elements

- Rattan, cane, carved wood, aged brass.
- Macramé, fringe, handwoven rugs, embroidered cushions.
- Ceramics and artisan-made objects.

## Gallery Walls and Displays

- Use mixed frame sizes with a loose perimeter line.
- Start with a central anchor piece.
- Maintain consistent spacing (typically 2–3 inches) even in eclectic layouts.

## Plant Strategy

Plants are a defining layer:

- Mix trailing, upright, and medium forms.
- Vary elevation with stands, shelves, and hanging planters.
- Keep grouping intentional rather than random.

## Organized Chaos Checklist

- Repeat 3–4 key colors through the room.
- Repeat at least one material (rattan, brass, dark wood) in multiple locations.
- Create clear focal points so the eye can rest.
- Leave circulation paths uncluttered.

## What to Avoid

- Confusing maximal quantity with composition.
- Too many unrelated bright colors.
- Every surface fully covered with objects.
- Ignoring storage; hidden storage is still essential in bohemian spaces.
