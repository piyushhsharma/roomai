# Maximalist Interior Design Guide

Maximalism is curated abundance, not random clutter. It embraces color, pattern, and collections while maintaining compositional structure.

## Core Philosophy

- More expression, not more chaos.
- Build visual storytelling through art, objects, and textiles.
- Create layered depth with repeated motifs and intentional contrast.

## Pattern Mixing Rules

- Mix scale: one dominant large-scale pattern, one medium, one small.
- Maintain a shared palette thread across mixed patterns.
- Ground complexity with solid-color surfaces.

## Color Confidence

- Use saturated hues deliberately: emerald, cobalt, ruby, marigold, plum.
- Balance with neutrals or deep grounding tones.
- Repeat accent colors in at least three locations for cohesion.

## Curated Collections

- Group objects by theme, color family, or material.
- Use shelves as edited compositions, not storage overflow.
- Rotate seasonal pieces to preserve visual freshness.

## Gallery Wall Best Practices

- Start from a central anchor.
- Keep spacing consistent.
- Mix orientation but preserve an overall boundary shape.
- Include breathing space around the entire gallery cluster.

## Maximalism vs Clutter

Maximalism has hierarchy:

- Primary focal point.
- Secondary supporting zones.
- Controlled negative space around key compositions.

Clutter lacks hierarchy and blocks function and movement.

## Rule of Odd Numbers

For decorative groupings, sets of 3 or 5 usually read more dynamic and natural than even-number clusters.

## Bold Wallpaper Use

- Prioritize one major wallpaper statement wall in medium rooms.
- In large spaces, balance wallpaper with plain upholstered surfaces.
- Coordinate, do not match, textiles to wallpaper colors.

## Common Mistakes

- Competing focal points in every direction.
- No consistent color backbone.
- Overscaled furniture plus heavy pattern everywhere.
- Ignoring storage and maintenance needs.
