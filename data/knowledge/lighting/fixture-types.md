# Lighting Fixture Types and Placement Rules

## Pendants vs Chandeliers vs Flush Mounts

- Pendant: directional emphasis, ideal above islands and dining tables.
- Chandelier: decorative focal fixture, best when centered over major social zone.
- Flush/semi-flush mount: practical for low ceilings and circulation areas.

Choose based on ceiling height, visual scale, and task needs.

## Reading Lamp Positioning

- Light source should come from slightly behind and beside shoulder line.
- Bottom of shade typically near seated eye level to reduce glare.
- Keep reachable switch location from seat/bed.

## Under-Cabinet Kitchen Lighting

- Place toward front edge of upper cabinets to reduce backsplash glare and improve counter illumination.
- Continuous LED strips often perform better than spaced pucks for uniformity.

## Picture and Art Lighting

- Aim at roughly 30-degree angle to reduce reflection hotspots.
- Use warm-neutral color temperature compatible with room palette.

## Floor Lamp Placement

- Place near seating corners to define conversation zones.
- Arc lamps can bridge over coffee table when side-table space is limited.
- Avoid placing lamps where traffic paths clip the base.

## Mirror Placement to Amplify Daylight

- Position mirrors opposite or adjacent to windows to bounce light.
- Keep reflected view intentional; avoid reflecting cluttered storage zones.

## Common Fixture Mistakes

- Oversized pendants in narrow circulation paths.
- Fixture hung too high over dining table (loses intimacy).
- No vertical lighting variation (all ceiling or all table lamps).
