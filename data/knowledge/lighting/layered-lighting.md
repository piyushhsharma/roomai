# Layered Lighting Fundamentals

Great interiors are lit in layers. Overhead-only lighting flattens depth, exaggerates shadows, and makes rooms feel clinical.

## Three-Layer System

### 1) Ambient (General)

Overall room illumination from ceiling fixtures, recessed lights, or cove lighting.

### 2) Task

Focused light for activities: reading, cooking, grooming, desk work.

### 3) Accent

Directional light for art, texture, architectural details, or mood.

All three are needed for a room to feel dynamic and usable day-to-night.

## Lumen Targets by Room (General Guide)

- Living room: moderate ambient with strong task/accent support.
- Bedroom: lower ambient + warm bedside task lights.
- Kitchen: high task brightness at counters + softer perimeter ambience.
- Home office: clear task light at desk + glare-controlled ambient fill.

Use dimmers to shift from functional to evening mood without changing fixtures.

## Color Temperature

- 2700K warm white: bedrooms, living spaces, hospitality mood.
- 3000K neutral-warm: kitchens, dining, multipurpose zones.
- 3500K–4000K: offices, utility areas needing visual acuity.

Avoid mixing very warm and very cool lamps in the same visual field.

## Natural Light Maximization

- Use mirror placement to bounce daylight deeper into rooms.
- Keep window treatments light-filtering where privacy allows.
- Avoid blocking windows with tall opaque furniture.

## Common Mistakes

- One central ceiling fixture only.
- No dimmers in social spaces.
- Task lights placed behind the user causing shadow.
- Too many high-K cool bulbs in relaxation zones.
