# Home Office Design Principles

## Desk Positioning

- Best: face a window with controlled glare or sit perpendicular to window.
- Avoid placing your back directly to the door when possible.
- Keep depth behind chair sufficient for movement and camera framing.

## Ergonomic Baselines

- Monitor approximately arm’s length away.
- Top third of screen around eye level.
- Elbows near 90 degrees at keyboard.
- Feet flat on floor or on footrest.

## Lighting for Screen Work

- Prioritize side lighting over frontal glare or overhead hotspot.
- Use layered desk task light plus ambient room fill.
- Keep monitor perpendicular to strongest daylight source when possible.

## Acoustic Improvements

- Add rugs, curtains, and upholstered surfaces to reduce echo.
- Use bookshelves or acoustic panels on reflective walls.
- Seal door gaps if speech privacy matters.

## Video Call Background Strategy

- Keep background intentional and uncluttered.
- Use one focal backdrop element (art, shelf vignette, plant).
- Add soft front/side key light to avoid harsh facial shadows.

## Productivity and Biophilia

- Place one to three plants within peripheral vision.
- Use green tones in moderate amounts for visual rest.
- Keep desktop surface mostly clear to reduce cognitive load.

## Common Mistakes

- Desk facing blank wall with no depth cues.
- Cool, high-intensity light causing fatigue.
- Decorative clutter within camera frame.
- No cable management, reducing visual calm and usable space.
