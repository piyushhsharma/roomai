# Bedroom Design Principles

## Bed Placement Rules

- Command position: bed should see the door without being directly in line with it.
- Prefer a solid wall behind the headboard.
- Avoid placing bed directly under a window when alternatives exist.
- Avoid foot of bed pointed straight at doorway when possible.

## Nightstand Sizing and Height

- Target nightstand top within about +/- 2 inches of mattress top height.
- Ensure sufficient width for practical use (lamp, glass, charging).

## Wardrobe and Storage Placement

- Preserve door swings and clear dressing path.
- Keep tall storage from visually overpowering bed wall.
- Use integrated storage in smaller rooms to reduce clutter.

## Zoning in Large Bedrooms

Create secondary zones:

- Reading corner.
- Vanity/work nook.
- Bench or low lounge area.

Use rugs and lighting to define each zone.

## Small Bedroom Illusion Techniques

- Use leggy furniture to reveal more floor area.
- Install wall-mounted sconces to free nightstand surface.
- Keep drapery near ceiling line to elongate wall height.
- Use mirror strategically to increase perceived depth.

## Common Mistakes

- Oversized bed frame for room dimensions.
- Too-dark palette with insufficient lighting.
- Mismatched bedside heights and lighting scale.
- Excessive decor over headboard creating visual heaviness.
