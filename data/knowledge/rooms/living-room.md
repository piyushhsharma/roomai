# Living Room Design Principles

## Focal Point Hierarchy

Identify the strongest anchor before arranging furniture:

1. Fireplace
2. TV wall
3. Exterior view
4. Artwork/feature wall

Competing focal points should be visually reconciled through alignment or hierarchy, not treated equally.

## Layout Patterns by Room Shape

### L-Shaped Rooms

- Anchor main seating in the largest rectangle.
- Use secondary leg for reading nook, game table, or media extension.

### Square Rooms

- Central conversation island often works best.
- Keep seating distances balanced on all sides.

### Long Narrow Rooms

- Break into two zones if length allows.
- Use rugs and lighting to signal zone boundaries.

## Conversation Area Sizing

- Keep primary seats close enough for natural conversation.
- Avoid pushing all furniture to perimeter walls.
- Include at least one side surface within reach of each primary seat.

## TV Mounting Height Rule

For comfortable viewing, TV center is often near seated eye line (commonly around 42 inches from finished floor, adjusted by sofa height and screen size).

## Open Plan Balancing

In open-plan living/dining:

- Use rugs to separate zones.
- Maintain consistent undertones across zones.
- Keep circulation paths explicit and unobstructed.

## Frequent Mistakes

- Tiny rug under large seating.
- Seating too far from one another.
- Over-prioritizing TV at the expense of social layout.
- No layered lighting around seating areas.
