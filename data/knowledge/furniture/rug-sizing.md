# Rug Sizing Rules for Interior Layout

Rug size determines whether furniture feels connected or fragmented. Undersized rugs are one of the most common design mistakes.

## Living Room Rules

### Option A: All Legs On

Best for larger rooms. All major seating legs sit fully on the rug.

### Option B: Front Legs On

Works in medium rooms. Front legs of sofa/chairs on rug, back legs off, but all pieces still visually tied to the rug footprint.

Avoid “postage stamp rugs” floating only under the coffee table.

## Bedroom Rug Placement

Typical strategy:

- Rug under lower two-thirds of bed.
- Extend 18–24 inches on each exposed side.

Alternative for small rooms:

- Two runners flanking the bed.

## Dining Room Formula

Rug size should equal table dimensions plus at least 24 inches on all sides so chairs remain on rug when pulled out.

## Layering Rugs

Use layering to add depth:

- Base: large neutral natural-fiber rug.
- Top: smaller patterned or plush rug in seating zone.

Keep top rug offset intentional, not centered by default.

## Common Sizing Mistakes

- Choosing rug based only on coffee table size.
- Not accounting for chair movement at dining table.
- Placing bedroom rug fully under bed with no side reveal.
- Ignoring room proportions (a huge rug can overwhelm tiny spaces if furniture is also oversized).
