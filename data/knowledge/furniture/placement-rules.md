# Furniture Placement Rules and Measurements

Good layout is measured, not guessed. Start with circulation and anchor pieces, then refine secondary items.

## Circulation Clearances

- Main pathways: minimum 36 inches (91 cm).
- Secondary pathways: minimum 24 inches (61 cm).
- Around kitchen islands and high-use zones, prefer 42+ inches where possible.

If circulation is tight, remove furniture before downsizing pathways.

## Sofa and Living Room Distances

- Sofa to coffee table: about 18 inches.
- Sofa to media wall/TV: commonly 36+ inches clear path behind circulation points.
- Maintain at least one clear movement path that does not cut through the conversation core.

## Floating Furniture

Avoid pushing all pieces to walls by default. In many rooms, floating furniture improves intimacy and scale.

- Pull sofa forward if room depth allows.
- Use console tables or slim storage behind floating sofas.
- Anchor with a properly sized rug.

## Anchor-First Sequence

1. Place focal anchor (sofa, bed, dining table).
2. Place second anchor (media unit, nightstands, sideboard).
3. Add circulation checks.
4. Add occasional pieces (chairs, side tables, lamps).

## Bedroom Hierarchy

- Bed is primary anchor.
- Nightstands and lighting define symmetry/asymmetry.
- Dresser/wardrobe placement follows door swing and walking paths.

## Dining Table Sizing Formula

Use room dimensions minus 6 feet (3 feet clearance each side) to estimate ideal max table size.

Example:

- Room length 15 ft -> table length should generally stay near or below 9 ft to preserve movement.

## Common Errors

- Oversized sectional in narrow room.
- Coffee table too far from sofa.
- Blocking primary routes with accent chairs.
- Ignoring door swing and drawer opening clearances.
