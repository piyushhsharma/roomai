# Room-Specific Color Guidance

## Bedroom

Best direction: calming, low-arousal tones.

- Recommended: soft blue, sage green, muted lavender, warm greige.
- Why: these hues reduce visual stress and support winding down.
- Avoid: highly saturated red, neon tones, high-contrast black-white schemes that feel alerting.

## Kitchen

Best direction: clean, energetic, appetite-friendly tones.

- Recommended: warm white, soft yellow accents, sage, muted olive, light greige.
- Why: bright but grounded colors improve perceived cleanliness and mood.
- Avoid: very dark wall colors in small kitchens (can suppress light), overly cool blue-greys with warm wood cabinets unless balanced.

## Living Room

Best direction: adaptable neutrals with one clear accent.

- Recommended: warm beige, greige, light taupe, soft stone + one accent (navy, forest green, terracotta).
- Why: living rooms serve multiple activities and need flexible color scaffolding.
- Avoid: too many accent colors competing at equal intensity.

## Home Office

Best direction: focus-supportive hues with moderate contrast.

- Recommended: muted blue, desaturated green, warm neutral base.
- Why: blue-green families can improve calm concentration.
- Avoid: high-saturation reds/oranges across large surfaces (often mentally fatiguing), pure white everywhere (glare).

## Bathroom

Best direction: spa-like clarity and softness.

- Recommended: warm white, soft blue-grey, pale stone, sage hints.
- Why: these tones pair well with water, tile, and reflective surfaces.
- Avoid: muddy mid-tone browns in low light, stark cool white with cool LED that feels clinical.

## Universal Rules

- Confirm undertones against fixed finishes first (tile, countertop, flooring).
- Evaluate colors in day and night lighting.
- Use darker tones in smaller doses if natural light is limited.
- Keep one neutral thread running through adjacent rooms for flow.
