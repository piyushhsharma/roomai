# Interior Color Theory and Palette Construction

## The 60-30-10 Rule (Detailed)

This classic ratio controls visual balance:

- 60% dominant color: walls, large rugs, major field.
- 30% secondary color: upholstery, curtains, casegoods, major textiles.
- 10% accent color: art, cushions, accessories, small decor.

Example (living room):

- 60% warm greige walls and large rug.
- 30% taupe sofa and oak tones.
- 10% deep teal in pillows, vases, and artwork details.

If accents exceed ~15–20%, the room often feels noisy.

## Undertones: Warm vs Cool

Two paints can look “white” but clash because of undertones.

- Warm undertones: yellow, red, peach base.
- Cool undertones: blue, green, violet base.

Rule: align undertones across fixed elements (flooring, countertop, tile, wall paint) before adding contrast colors.

## Natural Light and Color Perception

- North light: cooler, flatter; warm up with creamy neutrals.
- South light: warmer, brighter; can handle cooler neutrals.
- East light: warm morning, cooler afternoon.
- West light: neutral morning, warm golden evening.

Always test paint samples at different times of day on multiple walls.

## Harmony Systems

### Complementary

Opposites on the color wheel (blue/orange, red/green). High contrast, energetic. Use one as dominant and the other as controlled accent.

### Analogous

Neighbor colors (blue/blue-green/green). Calm, cohesive, ideal for bedrooms and spa-like spaces.

### Triadic

Three evenly spaced colors (e.g., blue/yellow/red variants). Vibrant but requires strong value control and neutral anchoring.

## Neutrals as Anchors

Neutrals are structural, not boring:

- They separate strong colors.
- They stabilize pattern-heavy schemes.
- They make material textures more legible.

## Accent Placement

For accents to feel intentional:

- Repeat each accent at least 2–3 times.
- Distribute vertically (low/mid/high in room) for visual rhythm.
- Keep accent clusters near focal areas.

## Paint Finish Selection

- Flat/Matte: hides wall imperfections; low washability.
- Eggshell: balanced finish for most living spaces.
- Satin: durable for kitchens, baths, kids’ areas.
- Semi-gloss/Gloss: trim, cabinetry, doors; high reflectance.

Use lower sheen on walls to avoid glare and color distortion under artificial light.
